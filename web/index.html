Overview

This dashboard estimates a low-altitude drone's intent — benign (commercial delivery), hobbyist (erratic recreational), or hostile (loiter / strike) — from its mechatronic flight signature. The core idea: digital telemetry (GPS, RF) can be spoofed, but actuator physics cannot. Sustained motor torque above the civilian envelope (100%) is the un-spoofable tell.

How the KAN was trained

A Kolmogorov–Arnold Network (KAN) — a compact, interpretable network — reads five per-drone time series (altitude, pitch angle, rotor RPM, velocity, torque load) plus the scene's swarm size, and outputs the intent class. Here is the training story in three figures:

1 · Class concept. Idealized signatures of each class. Hostile drones descend aggressively and push torque above the 100% envelope (dashed line); benign and hobbyist stay under it.
2 · Realistic data. Tracks are generated from continuous aggression/erraticness factors so the classes overlap (green→orange→red gradient). This avoids a misleadingly perfect score — the overlap is where mistakes happen.
3 · Result. Training loss converges without overfitting; the model reaches its test accuracy with errors only between adjacent classes (hostile↔hobbyist), as expected.

Using the dashboard

Simulate — Click Benign, Hobbyist, or Hostile to generate a random track of that type and see it classified. Tick hard / ambiguous for borderline cases the model finds genuinely difficult.
Configure (sliders) — Build a drone yourself: drag the sliders and the verdict, the charts, and the risk map update live. Push Torque Load above 100% and watch the intent shift toward hostile. You can also click the map to draw the flight path.
Upload CSV/JSON — Classify your own track. Use the sample.csv / sample.json templates for the exact format (one row per timestep), then drawing on the map still works.
Reading the output — The verdict + confidence and probability bars show the classification; the Why panel lists the features that drove it (and the torque-envelope status); the map shows a green→red risk density over real strategic sites; the charts show the five raw parameters over the flight.
